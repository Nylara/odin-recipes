Bread

by Brian Lagerstrom

Ingredients

  • 250 (1c) grams of warm water (approx 86F/30C)
  • 2 grams (2/3 tsp) of instant yeast
  • 300 grams of ripe poolish (see instructions below)
  • 12 grams (2 1/2tsp) of salt
  • 400 grams (3 1/3c) of bread flour
  • 50 grams (1/2c) of whole wheat flour

Method

Dough

  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the warm water, instant yeast, and ripe poolish.
  2. Add the salt, bread flour, and whole wheat flour to the bowl.
  3. Stir with a sturdy spoon until the ingredients are reasonably combined.
  4. Then use a wet hand to squeeze and massage the dough until there are no longer any dry spots and everything is evenly combined.
  5. Cover the bowl with a lid and let the dough ferment for 30 minutes.
  6. Perform a strength building fold by pulling out a handful of the dough on one side and folding it over to the other side of the dough. Rotate the bowl slightly and repeat about 7 more times, rotating the bowl after each fold.
  7. After the folds, grab the ball of dough, around its side/bottom, picking up the ball, rounding and rotating it, then setting it back down to tension across the top and round it onto a ball. Do this several times.
  8. Cover and allow to ferment at room temperature again for another 30 minutes. After 30 minutes repeat steps 6 and 7.
  9. Cover and allow the dough to ferment at room temperature for another 60 minutes. As this point you’ve fermented for 2 hours total. When you check back this time, the dough should have doubled in size.

Bread

  1. Flour your work surface and gently flip the dough out onto it.
  2. Flatten the dough slightly with a flat hand and pop any air bubbles.
  3. Shape the dough into a rough square.
  4. Cut the dough in half to create two equal rectangles.
  5. Transfer each loaf onto a parchment-lined sheet tray to proof.
  6. Cover with a sheet tray and let it proof for 60 minutes.
  7. Preheat the oven to 480°F (250°C).
  8. Bake the loaves for 20 minutes.
  9. After baking, let the loaves cool slightly before slicing with a bread knife.
